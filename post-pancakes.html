The Perfect Fluffy Pancakes — Recipe & Tips

There’s a small, simple joy to pancakes — a soft, warm stack that listens to syrup and berries. This is my go-to method for fluffy, reliable pancakes that taste like morning.

Ingredients (makes 8 pancakes)

  • 1 cup (120g) all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 cup (240ml) milk (dairy or plant)
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tbsp melted butter (plus more for the pan)
  • Optional: 1 tsp vanilla, 1/2 cup blueberries or chocolate chips

Method

  1. Mix dry ingredients in a bowl. In another bowl, whisk milk, egg, and melted butter.
  2. Pour wet into dry and gently fold until just combined. Small lumps are okay.
  3. Heat a nonstick pan over medium heat. Grease lightly. Pour ~1/4 cup batter per pancake.
  4. Cook until bubbles form and edges set (~2–3 min). Flip and cook 1–2 min more.
  5. Serve warm with butter, syrup, fruit, or yogurt.

Tips & Troubleshooting

Too dense? Don’t overmix. Let batter rest 5–10 minutes. Use fresh baking powder.

Burns outside but raw inside? Lower the heat and cook slightly longer.

Want fluffier pancakes? Separate the egg, whisk the egg white until soft peaks, fold into batter at the end.

Variations

Blueberry — fold berries into batter. Banana — add mashed banana and reduce milk slightly. Vegan — swap egg for 1 tbsp ground flax + 3 tbsp water and use plant milk.
