Baking Basics: Flour, Sugar, Leavening, and Temperature

Learning how ingredients behave is the fastest way to become confident in the kitchen.

Flour

All-purpose flour is most flexible. Bread flour has more protein (chewier). Cake flour is lower-protein (tender crumb). When measuring, spoon the flour into the cup and level it — don’t scoop directly.

Leavening

Baking powder and baking soda make things rise. Baking powder includes an acid; baking soda needs an acid to react. Use fresh leavening for best results (replace if older than ~6-12 months).

Temperature & Oven Tips

Oven thermostats can be off. Use an oven thermometer. For even browning, place pans in the center and avoid opening the door too often.

Sugar & Fats

Sugar tenderizes and browns; butter adds flavor and structure. When creaming butter and sugar, aim for light and airy for fluffy cakes.

Final advice

Start simple. Keep notes. Small changes yield big lessons — write down how a change affected the outcome.
